La Mer × Revieve

Turning a clinical AI skin-analysis result into a guided consultation that feels as reassuring as the in-store experience it's replacing.

Role
Lead UX designer
Company
Estée Lauder Companies
Partner
Revieve (AI skin analysis)
Surface
Post-analysis results page
Status
Shipped concept

The brief

An AI result that felt like a diagnosis, not a consultation

La Mer's digital skin consultation, powered by Revieve, generated genuinely useful, personalized skin analysis. The problem was what happened after: the results page presented Tone, Sensitivity, and Structure data in a way that felt clinical and overwhelming rather than the high-touch, reassuring experience customers get from an in-store consultation.

The challenge

Four specific gaps between the data and the experience

  • 01

    Results were presented in clinical, jargon-heavy language that overwhelmed unguided users instead of reassuring them.

  • 02

    Customers couldn't tell why a product was recommended for their specific skin, or how it fit into a daily routine.

  • 03

    There was no bridge between the emotional warmth of an in-store consultation and the online experience.

  • 04

    Negative framing around skin "concerns" undercut the empowering tone La Mer's brand is built on.

Approach

A workshop built around three questions

I ran a results-page workshop combining UX methods, affinity mapping, and brand storytelling with the La Mer team, anchored on:

  • 01

    How do we make results easy to navigate and understand on their own, without a consultant in the room?

  • 02

    How do we show the direct line between a skin concern and a specific La Mer product recommendation?

  • 03

    How do we build trust and credibility while keeping the experience luxurious, not clinical?

Competitive audits across luxury skincare confirmed the gap: most digital consultations defaulted to dashboards. None offered the guided, narrative feel customers associate with the in-store ritual.

UX decisions

Four moves that did the actual work

  • Guided experience

    Added ambassador-style storytelling and animated explainers for each skin concern, replacing raw data with narration.

  • Trust and transparency

    Linked every recommended product directly to the specific skin concern it addressed, with plain-language ingredient explanations.

  • Luxury tone

    Replaced clinical, negative-framed language with empowering copy and elegant, interactive visuals. No jargon.

  • Routine clarity

    Organized recommendations into clear core vs. extended regimens, showing exactly when and how to use each product.

Outcome

From a report to a relationship

The redesigned concept turned a data dump into a guided, educational, and reassuring journey. It closed the gap between La Mer's online experience and the in-store consultations it was meant to echo.
