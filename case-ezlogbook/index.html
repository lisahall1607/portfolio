Ezlogbook

A pilot logbook simple enough for non-aviators to follow, built from a blank page as the only designer on the project.

Role
Sole designer
Client
Ezlogbook
Users
Pilots & aviation students, 20–65
Year
2021–2022

The brief

Pilot logbooks were built for compliance, not people

Pilots and aviation students are required to log detailed flight data throughout their careers. It's not optional; it's how their hours and qualifications are proven. The existing tools for doing this were complicated, data-heavy, and slow, built around the regulatory requirement rather than the person filling it out.

Ezlogbook hired me to design a unique interface simple enough that even non-aviators could follow it, despite an end-user age range spanning 20 to 65, with very different relationships to technology.

Strategy

Equitable was the design constraint that mattered most

I anchored the design on four criteria (usable, equitable, enjoyable, useful), with particular weight on equitable, given how wide the age range was. The core move was replacing lengthy data-entry processes with simple icon-plus-text patterns, so the interface didn't assume a level of comfort with apps that not every pilot had.

The proposal: an app and website where pilots could register their logbook, log flights in as few steps as possible, and even upload medical documents and records, all without needing a manual to get started.

The work

From a data form to a one-step log

Outcome

The simplest logbook on the market

Ezlogbook is now known as the simplest pilot logbook available, and the only app on the market where pilots can log a flight in a single step.
